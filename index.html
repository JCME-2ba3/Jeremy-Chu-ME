About Me

My name is Jeremy Chu and welcome to my website.

I am a final year bachelor's student in Mechanical Engineering (Honours) and Biomedical Science at the University of Technology Sydney (UTS). I enjoy both degrees and love seeing how both can be combined. I have been privileged to have the opportunity of being a teaching assistant for an engineering statics class and co-lead of a $10,000 research grant into improving visibility and use of lab and workshop spaces.

I have interests in many different fields, including vibroacoustics research and the development of bioreactors and bioprocessing techniques. In 2025-26 I was fortunate to work with the UTS Centre for Audio Acoustics and Vibration (CAAV) where I simulated phenomena of and conducted experiments relating to pipe acoustics for leak detection.

I am also invested in the topic of Humanitarian Engineering. Where as part of my engineering honours project in 2024-25, I visited Timor-Leste for 2-weeks to study human-centred design, the importance of communication, and appropriate technology. This was in collaboration with the Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology (RMIT).

I aspire to continually improve and apply my skills and knowledge in a meaningful way for the benefit of society.
